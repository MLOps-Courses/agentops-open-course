# 0. Overview

This chapter is the "why and what" before the hands-on build. It explains what an AI agent is and how the agentic loop works, introduces the _AgentOps_ lifecycle that gives the course its shape, and tours the open-source ecosystem you will use — Google ADK, agentgateway, kagent, and the open standards MCP, A2A, and AGENTS.md. It also sets up your two practical choices: the language track (Python or Go) and the model provider (Gemini to start).

This chapter covers:

- **[0.0. Course](./0.0. Course.md)**: what, who, and why — prerequisites, time, cost, and how to use this course.
- **[0.1. Agents](./0.1. Agents.md)**: what an AI agent is, the agentic loop, common patterns, and when not to use one.
- **[0.2. AgentOps](./0.2. AgentOps.md)**: the AgentOps lifecycle and how MLOps, LLMOps, and AgentOps relate.
- **[0.3. Ecosystem](./0.3. Ecosystem.md)**: ADK, agentgateway, kagent, MCP, A2A, AGENTS.md, and the AAIF & CNCF.
- **[0.4. Languages](./0.4. Languages.md)**: Python vs Go for agents — strengths, weaknesses, and how to follow a track.
- **[0.5. Providers](./0.5. Providers.md)**: start on Gemini (API key/ADC); reach OpenAI, Anthropic, OpenRouter, and Ollama via the gateway later.
- **[0.6. Resources](./0.6. Resources.md)**: AI coding assistants and Agent Skills, community links, and a glossary.
